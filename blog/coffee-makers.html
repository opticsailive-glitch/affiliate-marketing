Buying Guide

When choosing a coffee maker, consider these factors:

  • Brewing Method: Drip, pour-over, French press, espresso, pod, or cold brew?
  • Capacity: How many cups do you typically brew at once?
  • Features: Programmable timers, temperature control, built-in grinders, milk frothers?
  • Ease of Cleaning: Removable parts, dishwasher-safe components, self-cleaning cycles?
  • Footprint: How much counter space will it require?
  • Budget: Quality coffee makers range from under $50 to over $1,000

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best brewing temperature for coffee?

The Specialty Coffee Association recommends brewing coffee between 195°F and 205°F (90-96°C) for optimal extraction. Temperatures below this range result in under-extracted, sour coffee, while temperatures above can cause over-extraction and bitterness.

Are pod coffee makers worth it?

Pod systems offer convenience and consistency but have ongoing costs for pods and generate more waste. They're best for those who prioritize convenience over cost savings and environmental impact.

How often should I clean my coffee maker?

You should clean removable parts after each use and perform a deep cleaning with descaling solution every 1-3 months, depending on your water hardness and usage frequency.
