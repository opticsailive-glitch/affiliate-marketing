Buying Guide

When choosing a home security camera, consider these factors:

  • Indoor vs Outdoor: Ensure outdoor cameras are weatherproof (IP65 or higher)
  • Video Quality: Look for at least 1080p resolution; 2K or 4K provides better detail
  • Night Vision: Infrared or color night vision for low-light conditions
  • Power Source: Wire-free for flexibility, wired for continuous power, or solar-powered
  • Storage: Local storage (SD card) vs cloud storage (often requires subscription)
  • Smart Features: Motion detection zones, person/vehicle/animal detection, two-way audio
  • Integration: Compatibility with Alexa, Google Assistant, or Apple HomeKit

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a subscription for home security cameras?

Many cameras offer free local storage options (via SD card) but require subscriptions for cloud storage and advanced features like person detection. Some brands like Eufy offer local storage with no mandatory fees.

Are wireless security cameras reliable?

Modern wireless security cameras are quite reliable, but their performance depends on your Wi-Fi signal strength. Consider Wi-Fi extenders or mesh systems if you have weak signals in certain areas of your property.

Can security cameras work without internet?

Some cameras can record locally to an SD card without internet, but you'll lose remote viewing, alerts, and smart features. For full functionality, an internet connection is typically required.
